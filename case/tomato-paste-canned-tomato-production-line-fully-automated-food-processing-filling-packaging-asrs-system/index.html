Summary

This case study presents a fully automated tomato processing production line covering fresh tomato washing, sorting, pulping, concentration, sterilization, filling, sealing, labeling, packaging, palletizing, and automated warehouse integration.

The system is designed for high-volume food manufacturing plants producing tomato paste, ketchup, canned tomatoes, and other tomato-based products with strict hygiene and continuous production requirements.

Technology

  • Industrial food processing system
  • Automated washing and sorting system
  • Pulping and separation technology (skin & seed removal)
  • Vacuum evaporation concentration system
  • Continuous sterilization and thermal control system
  • Automatic can/bottle filling and sealing line
  • Retort sterilization system
  • Cooling and drying conveyor system
  • Automatic labeling and coding system
  • Weight checking and quality inspection system
  • Cartoning and palletizing automation
  • ASRS automated warehouse integration system

Challenge

Traditional tomato processing factories face several challenges:

High manual labor dependency in washing and sorting
Inconsistent product quality due to unstable processing conditions
Food safety and hygiene control difficulties
Inefficient sterilization and temperature management
Low production continuity and bottlenecks
Limited automation in packaging and logistics
Inefficient warehouse and inventory management

These issues limit scalability and global competitiveness in food manufacturing.

Solution

A fully automated tomato processing system integrates the entire production chain:

Continuous washing, sorting, and preparation system
Automated pulping and refining process
Controlled evaporation and concentration system
Sterilization and aseptic filling technology
Fully automated packaging and labeling line
Intelligent quality inspection system
Robotic palletizing system
ASRS automated warehouse integration

This ensures stable, hygienic, and continuous food production.

Workflow & Layout

Fresh tomatoes are received and washed automatically
Sorting system removes defective raw materials
Crushing and pulping system extracts tomato juice
Separation system removes skin and seeds
Evaporation system concentrates tomato paste
Sterilization system ensures food safety
Filling system packs product into cans or bottles
Sealing and cooling system stabilizes packaging
Labeling, coding, and inspection system completes packaging
Cartoning and palletizing system prepares shipment
ASRS warehouse system stores finished products

Results & ROI

  • Production efficiency significantly increased
  • Labor costs reduced across entire production chain
  • Food safety and hygiene standards improved
  • Continuous 24/7 production capability achieved
  • Packaging accuracy and consistency improved
  • Warehouse logistics fully automated
  • Reduced contamination risk
  • Improved overall supply chain efficiency

Equipment List

  • Conveyor and material handling system

Project Overview / Opening

This project demonstrates a fully integrated tomato processing and packaging production line designed for high-efficiency food manufacturing.

The system combines food processing technology, automation control, and intelligent logistics to achieve end-to-end production automation.

Key Points

  • Strict hygiene and food safety control design
  • Continuous production with stable temperature management
  • Integrated packaging and labeling automation
  • Robotics-based palletizing system
  • Seamless ASRS warehouse integration
  • High scalability for large food factories
