Challenge

Food manufacturers often struggle with equipment planning due to:

Lack of complete machine list for full production line
Unclear system boundaries between processing, packaging, and logistics
Over-purchasing or missing key equipment
Poor compatibility between different machine suppliers
Inefficient factory layout due to incorrect equipment selection
Difficulty estimating total investment cost

These issues lead to inefficient plant design and production bottlenecks.

Solution

A complete tomato paste production line should be designed as an integrated system:

Raw material handling and washing systems
Core processing and concentration equipment
Sterilization and aseptic filling systems
Packaging and labeling automation
Quality inspection and control systems
Centralized PLC/MES control system
Warehouse and logistics automation (ASRS integration)
Modular and scalable factory design

This ensures full-process coordination and stable production.

12.Robotic palletizing system
13.ASRS warehouse storage system

Project Overview / Opening

A tomato paste production line is not a single machine but a complete system composed of interconnected food processing and automation equipment.

Understanding the full equipment breakdown is essential for building an efficient, scalable, and profitable food factory.

Key Points

  • Production lines are system-based, not machine-based
  • Equipment compatibility determines production stability
  • Automation improves overall factory efficiency
  • Packaging and logistics are as important as processing
  • ASRS integration enhances long-term scalability
  • Proper equipment selection reduces investment risk
  • PLC/MES systems enable full production control

Implementation / Workflow

3.Equipment selection and supplier coordination
4.Factory layout and infrastructure planning
5.Automation system integration design
6.Installation and commissioning
7.Packaging and logistics system setup
8.Final system testing and optimization
