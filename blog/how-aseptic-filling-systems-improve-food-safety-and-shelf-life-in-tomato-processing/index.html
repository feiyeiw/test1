Challenge

Traditional food filling systems face critical issues:

High risk of microbial contamination during packaging
Limited shelf life of tomato paste and sauces
Inconsistent sterilization across production batches
Exposure to air and external environment
High dependency on preservatives
Difficulty meeting international food safety standards
Limited automation in hygienic control

These challenges reduce product quality and global export competitiveness.

Solution

Aseptic filling systems solve these challenges by creating a fully sterile production environment:

Closed sterile filling environment prevents contamination
High-temperature sterilization ensures microbial elimination
UHT and thermal control systems stabilize product safety
Automated filling reduces human contact
CIP/SIP systems maintain equipment hygiene
Integrated sealing ensures airtight packaging
Continuous monitoring ensures process stability

This enables long shelf life without preservatives.

Workflow & Layout

Sterilization of product via UHT or thermal system
Transfer into sterile buffer tank
Product enters aseptic filling chamber
Sterile containers are automatically positioned
Filling occurs in sealed sterile environment
Automatic sealing prevents air exposure
Cooling and stabilization process
Labeling and packaging system
Cartoning and palletizing
Transfer to ASRS warehouse system

Results & ROI

  • Shelf life significantly extended (6–24 months depending on product)
  • Food safety risk reduced dramatically
  • Product quality consistency improved
  • Reduced need for chemical preservatives
  • Higher export compliance capability
  • Lower product recall risk
  • Increased brand trust and market value
  • Higher ROI from premium-grade packaging
